# Content Creation Guide for Therapists and Admins

## Overview
SafeSpace's content management system allows therapists and administrators to create, publish, and manage educational articles and resources for the community. This guide covers the entire content creation workflow.

## Getting Started

### Who Can Create Content?
- **Therapists:** Can create articles related to their expertise
- **Administrators:** Can create any type of content and manage all articles
- **Content Reviewers:** Can review and approve submitted content

### Content Types
- **Educational Articles:** Mental health information, coping strategies, guides
- **Resource Lists:** Helpful tools, books, websites, apps
- **Activity Guides:** Therapeutic exercises, mindfulness practices
- **FAQ Articles:** Common questions and answers
- **News Updates:** Platform updates, community highlights

## Content Creation Process

### Step 1: Planning Your Content

#### Choose Your Topic
- **Identify Needs:** What questions do your clients frequently ask?
- **Check Existing Content:** Avoid duplicating existing articles
- **Consider Your Audience:** Children, guardians, therapists, or all?
- **Focus on Value:** What specific help will this provide?

#### Research and Preparation
- **Gather Sources:** Use reputable mental health resources
- **Check Guidelines:** Follow SafeSpace content standards
- **Plan Structure:** Outline your main points
- **Collect Materials:** Images, links, references

### Step 2: Accessing the Content Editor

#### For Therapists:
1. Log into your SafeSpace account
2. Navigate to "Content" in the main menu
3. Click "Create New Article"

#### For Administrators:
1. Go to "Admin Dashboard"
2. Select "Content Management"
3. Click "Create New Article"

### Step 3: Writing Your Article

#### Article Information
**Title:**
- Keep it clear and descriptive
- Use keywords your audience would search for
- Aim for 50-60 characters for best display

**Excerpt:**
- Write a compelling 2-3 sentence summary
- This appears in article lists and search results
- Should entice readers to click and read more

**Target Audience:**
- **Children:** Age-appropriate language and concepts
- **Guardians:** Parent-focused guidance and support
- **Therapists:** Professional development and techniques
- **All:** General mental health information

#### Content Writing

**Structure Your Article:**
1. **Introduction:** Hook the reader and preview what they'll learn
2. **Main Content:** Break into clear sections with headings
3. **Practical Tips:** Include actionable advice
4. **Conclusion:** Summarize key points and next steps

**Writing Best Practices:**
- **Clear Language:** Avoid jargon, explain technical terms
- **Short Paragraphs:** Keep paragraphs to 3-4 sentences
- **Active Voice:** Use active rather than passive voice
- **Inclusive Language:** Be mindful of diverse backgrounds
- **Empathetic Tone:** Show understanding and compassion

#### Using the Rich Text Editor

**Formatting Options:**
- **Headings:** Use H2, H3 for section organization
- **Bold/Italic:** Emphasize important points
- **Lists:** Use bullet points and numbered lists
- **Links:** Add relevant external resources
- **Images:** Include helpful visuals (with proper licensing)

**Editor Features:**
- **Auto-save:** Your work is saved automatically
- **Preview:** See how your article will look to readers
- **Word Count:** Track article length (aim for 800-2000 words)
- **Spell Check:** Built-in spelling and grammar assistance

### Step 4: Adding Metadata

#### Categories and Tags
**Categories:**
- Anxiety and Stress
- Depression and Mood
- Family Relationships
- Child Development
- Coping Strategies
- Therapeutic Techniques
- Crisis Support
- General Wellness

**Tags:**
- Use 3-5 relevant tags
- Include specific techniques or conditions
- Think about what users might search for
- Examples: "mindfulness," "teenagers," "communication"

#### SEO and Discovery
**Meta Description:**
- Write a 150-160 character description
- Include your main keyword
- Make it compelling for search results

**Featured Image:**
- Upload a relevant, high-quality image
- Ensure you have rights to use the image
- Alt text for accessibility

### Step 5: Review and Submit

#### Self-Review Checklist
- [ ] Title is clear and engaging
- [ ] Content is well-organized with headings
- [ ] Information is accurate and evidence-based
- [ ] Language is appropriate for target audience
- [ ] All links work and are relevant
- [ ] Images are properly attributed
- [ ] Spelling and grammar are correct
- [ ] Article provides genuine value

#### Submission Process
1. **Save as Draft:** Save your work before submitting
2. **Preview:** Review how it will appear to readers
3. **Submit for Review:** Send to the moderation queue
4. **Track Status:** Monitor approval progress

## Content Moderation Process

### Review Stages

#### 1. Automatic Checks
- **Spam Detection:** Automated filtering for spam content
- **Plagiarism Check:** Verification of original content
- **Safety Screening:** Check for harmful or inappropriate content

#### 2. Editorial Review
- **Content Quality:** Accuracy, clarity, and usefulness
- **Guidelines Compliance:** Adherence to SafeSpace standards
- **Audience Appropriateness:** Suitable for intended audience
- **Professional Standards:** Meets therapeutic and ethical guidelines

#### 3. Final Approval
- **Admin Review:** Final check by content administrators
- **Publication:** Article goes live on the platform
- **Notification:** Author receives approval confirmation

### Review Timeline
- **Standard Articles:** 2-3 business days
- **Urgent Content:** 24 hours (mark as urgent when submitting)
- **Complex Topics:** Up to 5 business days for thorough review

## Managing Your Published Content

### Content Dashboard
**View Your Articles:**
- See all your published, pending, and draft articles
- Track performance metrics
- Access editing tools
- Monitor reader engagement

**Performance Metrics:**
- **Views:** How many people have read your article
- **Engagement:** Comments, ratings, bookmarks
- **Shares:** How often it's been shared
- **Search Performance:** How people find your content

### Updating Published Content

#### When to Update:
- **New Information:** Research updates or new techniques
- **Broken Links:** Fix outdated or non-working links
- **User Feedback:** Address questions or concerns in comments
- **Seasonal Relevance:** Update for current events or seasons

#### Update Process:
1. Open your published article
2. Click "Edit Article"
3. Make necessary changes
4. Submit for re-review (if major changes)
5. Minor updates may go live immediately

### Responding to Reader Engagement

#### Comments Management
- **Monitor Comments:** Check regularly for new comments
- **Respond Professionally:** Answer questions and provide clarification
- **Moderate Appropriately:** Flag inappropriate comments
- **Encourage Discussion:** Ask follow-up questions

#### Ratings and Feedback
- **Review Ratings:** See how readers rate your content
- **Read Feedback:** Learn from constructive criticism
- **Improve Future Content:** Apply lessons learned
- **Thank Readers:** Acknowledge positive feedback

## Content Guidelines and Standards

### Quality Standards

#### Accuracy Requirements
- **Evidence-Based:** Use peer-reviewed sources when possible
- **Current Information:** Ensure information is up-to-date
- **Professional Standards:** Follow therapeutic best practices
- **Fact-Checking:** Verify all claims and statistics

#### Writing Standards
- **Clarity:** Write in clear, accessible language
- **Structure:** Use logical organization and flow
- **Completeness:** Cover topics thoroughly but concisely
- **Originality:** Create original content, properly cite sources

### Content Restrictions

#### Prohibited Content
- **Medical Advice:** Don't provide specific medical diagnoses or treatments
- **Crisis Intervention:** Don't attempt to handle crisis situations through articles
- **Personal Information:** Don't share client details or personal stories without permission
- **Promotional Content:** Don't use articles primarily for self-promotion

#### Sensitive Topics
- **Suicide and Self-Harm:** Follow specific guidelines for these topics
- **Trauma:** Handle with extra care and appropriate warnings
- **Medication:** Provide general information only, refer to professionals
- **Legal Issues:** Don't provide legal advice

### Ethical Considerations

#### Professional Boundaries
- **Scope of Practice:** Stay within your professional expertise
- **Dual Relationships:** Avoid conflicts of interest
- **Confidentiality:** Protect client privacy at all times
- **Cultural Sensitivity:** Be aware of diverse backgrounds and experiences

#### Child Safety
- **Age-Appropriate Content:** Ensure content is suitable for intended age groups
- **Safety First:** Prioritize child safety in all recommendations
- **Parental Involvement:** Encourage appropriate guardian involvement
- **Reporting Requirements:** Understand mandatory reporting obligations

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

#### "Article Stuck in Review"
**Possible Causes:**
- High volume of submissions
- Complex topic requiring extra review
- Technical issues with the system

**Solutions:**
- Wait for standard review timeline
- Contact support if urgent
- Check for any requested revisions

#### "Editor Not Saving Changes"
**Solutions:**
- Check internet connection
- Try refreshing the page
- Copy content to clipboard as backup
- Contact technical support

#### "Images Not Uploading"
**Common Causes:**
- File size too large (max 5MB)
- Unsupported file format
- Browser compatibility issues

**Solutions:**
- Resize images before uploading
- Use JPG, PNG, or GIF formats
- Try a different browser
- Clear browser cache
